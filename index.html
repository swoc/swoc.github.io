Introduction

Hello reader! Nice of you to drop by.

My name is Andres. I was born in 1990 in Estonia where I mostly live to date.

I got my first computer in 1997, soon after that I started coding – my first experiments were done with Pascal, VBS, HTML. After I had built a few games in Pascal, I discovered Game Maker and managed to put together a few games with that program as well.

For some reason I stopped working with games and went back to web design – after we got a dial-up connection at home, I started using IRC and the kind people on the internet suggested looking into PHP, which I did. At first, it didn't make any sense, but slowly I started to get along with it better.

At this point in my life my mom ordered me to spend less time sitting behind screens and suggested me to go play outside instead – so my programming adventures and relations with computers were put on hold for a while.

Instead of going outside I spent my time tinkering with electronics. Soon after that I decided to look into PICs – that meant programming in ASM (my first ever PIC project was a 3x3x3 LED cube). As I realised that I in fact hated ASM, I went along and bought my first AVR and started coding in Embedded C and C++.

That somehow got me interested in this field and I started learning new things – as soon as I brought my first ever Android phone (Xperia Arc S (L18i)) I picked up Java – as it looked horrible to me at that time, I decided to do all my software developing in Python and C.

Embedded systems, Robotics and Electronics are subjects I am deeply enthralled with to this day. I have built custom electric solutions for several rally cars, developed my own tachometer and instrument panel, and built many educational / testing devices for the Estonian University of Life Sciences and more. I even started the robotics club in the Estonian University of Life Sciences.

As you may see I am a guy with many talents: I am strong-willed – if I don't know something yet, I'll look it up and learn it. I am persistent – if I am into something, I will stick it out till the end. I will not quit – I still have some projects even from my childhood that are “haunting” me because I could not solve / build them as good as I would have liked to back then, and so I rebuild / rediscover projects and things from time to time and try to make them perfect.

Sidenote - it all started from computer games and aside of playing some every once in a while, I still do have my own computer games projects.
